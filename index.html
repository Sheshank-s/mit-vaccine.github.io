The MIT   Vaccines for All   Conference

December 11, 2020 | 9:00 AM EST - 12:00 PM EST

Register

The MIT "Vaccines for All" Conference addresses challenges designing digital solutions which engage citizens in four core areas â€” logistics, health outcomes, user-centric impact, and communication.

Disparities in equitable distribution, vaccine efficacy, duration of immunity, multi-dose adherence, and privacy-focused user experience are among the most critical difficulties that must be addressed. The biggest hurdles in designing digital solutions which engage citizens in this pandemic are human and societal as well as technological. Trust at all levels and among all participants is mandatory for success. Can participatory real time epidemiology and user-centric crowd-sourced solutions help?

The conference will bring together experts and stakeholders to address the consequences impacting disease-spread, individual behaviour, society, the economy, and data privacy.

The MIT Vaccines for All Conference invites poster submissions in all areas related (but not limited to), vaccine logistics, health outcomes, challenges, and consequences. To create a constructive, innovative, and open community, all high-quality posters will be welcomed to present at the poster session. Presenting a poster is a great way to receive feedback for both published and unpublished work. We highly encourage posters based on published work. Poster presentations are an integral part of the conference and will be presented in interactive sessions. Additionally, posters will be distributed to all conference participants and virtually presented at 1 PM ET on December 11th. We look forward to an exceptionally diverse, inclusive, and an interesting poster session.

Due to the virtual nature of the poster presentation this year, we recommend posters be submitted in landscape format.
