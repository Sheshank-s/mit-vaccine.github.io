Vaccines for All

COVID-19 Digital Tools to Empower Citizens

December 11, 2020 | 9:00 AM EST - 2:00 PM EST

Register

Designing digital solutions which engage citizens in four core areas — logistics, health outcomes, user-centric impact, and communication.

Speakers

Disparities in equitable distribution, vaccine efficacy, duration of immunity, multi-dose adherence, and privacy-focused user experience are among the most critical difficulties that must be addressed. The biggest hurdles in designing digital solutions which engage citizens in this pandemic are human and societal as well as technological. Trust at all levels and among all participants is mandatory for success. Can participatory real time epidemiology and user-centric crowd-sourced solutions help? The deployment of exposure notification/health passport apps is a promising start.



The conference will bring together experts and stakeholders to address the consequences impacting disease-spread, individual behaviour, society, the economy, and data privacy.

Call for Spotlight

  • Presentations
    •  
  • Essays

The Conference on "Vaccines for All" hosted by MIT Media Lab, AutoID Lab and New America invites abstract submissions in all areas related (but not limited to): vaccine logistics, health outcomes, challenges, and consequences. To create a constructive, innovative, and open community, all quality abstracts will be welcomed to present at the presentation session. Presenting an abstract is a great way to receive feedback for both published and unpublished work. We highly encourage abstract based on published work. Abstract presentations are an integral part of the conference and will be presented in interactive sessions. Additionally, abstracts will be distributed to all conference participants and virtually presented at 1 PM ET on December 11th.

Abstracts should be limited to maximum of 4 pages (single column) for technical content including figures and possible references, and with one additional optional 5th page containing only references.

Due to the virtual nature of the conference, we also accept abstracts for both published and ongoing work be in the form of slides presentation format.


The Conference on "Vaccines for All" hosted by MIT Media Lab, AutoID Lab and New America invites essay submissions pertinent to vaccination and the COVID-19 global pandemic. We highly encourage everyone to seize this opportunity to learn, write, and discuss about COVID-19 vaccination. We recommend utilizing sources to help you in your research, but make sure to use your imagination and think outside the box! Essays can expand into topics such as:

  • Managing the logistical complications of vaccine delivery over time
  • Resolving technological barriers in developing/underdeveloped socio-economic regions while providing mechanisms that ensure vaccine delivery
  • Creating privacy-centric systems to verify if an individual has received a vaccine

Deadline for submission: December 10
Submission Instructions: http://pathfinders.mit.edu
Essay Template: Latex and Word
